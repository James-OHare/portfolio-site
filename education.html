Outside the Terminal

Life & Interests

Software engineering is what I do, not all of who I am. A few things that keep me grounded.

Distance Running

I run four to five times a week, mostly along the canal or out along the Wicklow Way at weekends. I completed the Dublin City Marathon in 2023 (3:41) and am training toward sub-3:30 in 2024. Running is where I do my best thinking.

Espresso & Coffee

I roast my own beans on a Behmor 1600 and pull shots on a Decent DE1. The intersection of precise measurement, temperature stability, and chaos is a useful reminder that engineering problems exist outside of software too.

Reading

I read broadly — distributed systems papers, philosophy of mind, contemporary Irish fiction. Some recent favourites: Sally Rooney's Intermezzo, Kleppmann's DDIA, and Derek Parfit's Reasons and Persons.

Open Source

I maintain several open-source projects and contribute to others in the Go and TypeScript ecosystems. I believe the long-term health of software infrastructure depends on people building things without immediate commercial incentive.

Music

I play guitar badly and piano slightly less badly. Largely jazz and classical. I have an ongoing project to learn the Goldberg Variations, which is going poorly but enjoyably.

Dublin

Born and raised in Dublin. The city has changed enormously in the six years since I graduated — the tech industry's growth has made it a strange and fascinating place to be a software engineer. I'm proud to be part of it.
